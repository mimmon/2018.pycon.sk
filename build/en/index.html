PyCon will come to Slovakia in 2018 again.

Third year will happen 9-11. March, 2018 in Bratislava.

PyCon SK is an international conference organized by the community of enthusiasts and supporters of the Python programming language. It is designed for anyone who works professionally and also for anyone who is learning this programming language. Conference is held in English and (Czech) Slovak language. If you don't know any Slovak or Czech, don't worry, there will be stuff for you to do.

You can import conference schedule as a calendar to your favorite calendar app. This way you'll be always up-to-date with latest program, even offline. In case of program change calendar will update automatically. Get event calendar: pyconsk.ics

Talks and speakers list has been published on our website. Soon we'll publish also the time schedule.

We have received almost 100 high quality talks and workshops proposals! It will take some time to go through all of them so please be patient. We have started to publishing some of the speakers on our website.

17.1.2018: PyCon SK 2018 under the patronage of the president of the Slovak Republic.

We are excited that for the third time in the row, PyConSK will be held under the auspices of the president of the Slovak Republic. We are committed to bring you another conference of excellent quality which will represent the Slovak Republic in dignity before audiences from all over Europe and the rest of the world.

07.12.2017: Download PyCon SK 2018 Conference press release.

01.09.2017: We are preparing a section for Informatics Teachers to be held during the PyCon SK Conference.

The aim of this section is to teach teachers the benefits and possibilities of using Python in teaching. We invite teachers who are teaching Python to share their experiences with conference attendees and submit a talk proposal.

01.09.2017: We are pleased to announce a DevOps track at PyCon SK 2018.

DevOps (a portmanteau of "development" and "operations") is a software engineering practice that aims to unify software development (Dev) and software operation (Ops). The main emphasis of the DevOps movement is to strongly advocate for automation and monitoring at all steps of software production, from integration, testing, releasing to deployment and infrastructure management.
If you think you have something to say on this topic, you are most welcome to submit your CfP. We hope to get the error budget right and to see developers and admins working towards the same goals.
